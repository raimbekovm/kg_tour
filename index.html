About the Route

An Immersive Journey Through the Heart of Central Asia

Designed for adventurous travelers aged 25–55, this route takes you from the bustling bazaars of Bishkek to the ancient Silk Road caravanserais of the Tien Shan. Experience nomadic culture, dramatic landscapes, and authentic communities—all with zero internal flights and a deep commitment to responsible travel.

Culinary Journey

Taste the Silk Road

Every meal tells a story. From nomadic feasts in felt yurts to Uzbek family kitchens — the route is a journey through Central Asian gastronomy.

Day 1

Beshbarmak

Kyrgyz • National dish

Horse or lamb meat served on hand-pulled noodles with onion broth. Eaten with hands (“besh barmak” = five fingers). The quintessential nomadic feast.

Day 4

Ashlyamfu & Lagman

Dungan & Uyghur • Karakol

Ashlyamfu: cold noodle soup with starch jelly and spicy vinegar sauce. Lagman: hand-pulled noodles in rich tomato-lamb broth. Two cultures, one city.

Day 5

Shorpo & Kumys

Nomadic • Yurt lunch

Hearty mutton broth with potatoes and carrots, paired with kumys — fermented mare's milk, slightly fizzy and sour. An authentic shepherd's lunch at 2,500m.

Day 6

Kymyz Evening

Nomadic • Son-Kul

An evening in a yurt at 3,016m. Beshbarmak from fresh lamb, bowls of kymyz, boorsoki (fried bread), and kaymak (clotted cream) under a canopy of stars.

Day 8

Plov & Samsa

Uzbek • Arslanbob

Cauldron-cooked pilaf (rice, lamb, carrots, chickpeas) with tandoor-baked samsa pastries. Home-cooked by your Uzbek host family with local walnuts and herbs.

Day 9

Uzgen Plov

Uzbek • Uzgen

A regional masterpiece: made with local Uzgen rice (dark, fragrant, and slightly sticky). Unlike Fergana plov, it uses a drier technique and is considered the finest in the region.

Before You Go

Practical Information

Everything you need to know to prepare for your 10-day Kyrgyz adventure.

📅

Best Season

June through September. Son-Kul yurt camps open mid-June to mid-September only. July–August offers the warmest weather and driest trails. September brings golden autumn colors and walnut harvest in Arslanbob.

🏔

Difficulty Level

Moderate. Trekking at altitudes up to 3,200m. Longest trek: 7 km (Ala-Archa). Long driving days (up to 9h on Day 8). Moderate fitness required.

🎒

What to Pack

  • Layered clothing (0°C at Son-Kul nights)
  • Sturdy hiking boots (broken in)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ & sunglasses
  • Reusable water bottle (provided)
  • Headlamp for yurt camps
  • Power bank (limited charging)
💰

Budget Estimate

Approximately $80–120/day including all accommodation, meals, transport, guides, and workshop fees. International flights not included. Kyrgyzstan is visa-free for 60+ countries.

📱

Connectivity

Mobile coverage is good in cities and along Issyk-Kul. No signal at Son-Kul, Tash-Rabat, and mountain passes. Buy a local SIM (Megacom or Beeline) at Bishkek airport for ~$5. Wi-Fi available at hotels.

🚌

Transport

Shared minibus for the entire group (8–12 people). No internal flights — the overland route is part of the experience. Total distance: ~1,500 km across Kyrgyzstan's most scenic roads.
